4. What was the primary cause of the Dust Bowl during the 1930s?
a) Excessive rainfall and flooding
*b) Severe drought combined with poor farming practices
c) Widespread wildfires across the Great Plains
d) An invasion of agricultural pests
feedback: While drought was a factor, the Dust Bowl was largely a man-made disaster. Decades of deep plowing and removal of native prairie grasses left topsoil exposed. When the drought hit, there was nothing to hold the soil in place, leading to massive dust storms.
